# Tesla-Coil

---

## 1. Abstract

This project presents the design and construction of a small-scale Tesla Coil used to generate high-voltage, low-current electrical discharges.

The goal of the project was to understand the principles of electromagnetic induction, resonance, and high-frequency transformers in a practical and visual way.

---

## 2. Motivation

We chose this project because we were interested in high-voltage physics and wanted to see how electromagnetic fields can be used to produce visible electrical discharges in air.

Unlike our other robotics projects, this one focuses more on physics and electrical engineering concepts rather than control systems.

---

## 3. Objectives

- Build a functional low-power Tesla Coil  
- Understand electromagnetic induction and resonance  
- Study high-frequency transformer behavior  
- Visualize electrical discharge in air  
- Learn safety principles in high-voltage systems  

---

## 4. System Overview

The Tesla Coil is composed of two main circuits:

### Primary Circuit
- Capacitor  
- Primary coil  
- Switching component (driver circuit)  

### Secondary Circuit
- Secondary coil with many windings  
- Top load (toroid or metal sphere)  

The interaction between these two circuits creates a resonant electromagnetic field.

---

## 5. Hardware Components

- High-voltage power supply (low current)  
- Primary copper coil  
- Secondary coil (fine wire winding)  
- MOSFET / transistor switching stage  
- Capacitors  
- Toroid (top load)  
- Insulating base structure  

---

## 6. Working Principle

The Tesla Coil operates using electromagnetic resonance.

1. The primary circuit generates a rapidly oscillating current  
2. This creates a changing magnetic field  
3. The secondary coil picks up this energy through induction  
4. Voltage is amplified significantly due to resonance  
5. Electrical discharges occur in the air near the top load  

---

## 7. Observations

- Visible high-voltage arcs were generated at the top load  
- Strong electromagnetic field effects were observed nearby  
- The system is highly sensitive to tuning and coil alignment  

---

## 8. Challenges

- Difficult tuning between primary and secondary resonance  
- Heat generation in switching components  
- Electrical losses due to imperfect coil design  
- Stability of oscillation frequency  

---

## 9. Solutions

- Adjusted coil spacing and winding parameters  
- Improved switching efficiency  
- Fine-tuned capacitor values for better resonance  
- Reinforced insulation for safety  

---

## 10. Safety Considerations

This project involves high voltage, even at low current levels.

We took precautions such as:
- Keeping a safe distance during operation  
- Using proper insulation materials  
- Avoiding direct contact with the coil during operation  
- Limiting operating time to prevent overheating  

---

## 11. What We Learned

- How electromagnetic induction works in practice  
- The importance of resonance in energy transfer  
- How high-frequency circuits behave differently from DC systems  
- Why safety is critical in high-voltage electronics  

---

## 12. Limitations

- Not optimized for continuous operation  
- Low power output compared to professional Tesla coils  
- Highly sensitive to component variations  
- Requires careful manual tuning  

---

## 13. Future Improvements

- More stable driver circuit (ZVS design)  
- Better thermal management  
- Improved coil winding precision  
- Adjustable frequency control system  
- Increased efficiency and arc stability  

---

## 14. Conclusion

This project provided a practical introduction to high-voltage physics and electromagnetic resonance.

Even though it is a simplified version of a Tesla Coil, it demonstrates key principles of energy transfer and high-frequency electrical behavior.
